Storytelling with visualization experiment

You will see a series of charts that tell a story. After each step, we will ask you some questions about the charts and story.

We expect your answers to be between about 3 and 6 sentences long. The whole experiment typically takes around one hour. The sequence will contain 10 pages and a short survey at the end.

Below you can see an example of a chart. One set of answers is something we would consider good. The other answer is something we would consider bad. We will refuse HITs with 3 or more bad answers.

Example visualization

Example of two good answers:

What is shown on each axis of this chart?

Y: International aid donations in billion USD, X: Years

What can you conclude from this chart?

Over time new countries have started donating to international aid. Between 2005 and 2010 there has been a sharp peak of donations because South Korea and India have started to donate large sums. After 2010 many countries have strongly reduced their donations. This could be a result of of the financial crisis.

Example of a bad answer:

What can you conclude from this chart?

countries and international aid donations
